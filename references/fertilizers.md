# Fertilizers Reference

## Fertilizer types

| Type | How it works | Duration | Best for |
|------|-------------|----------|----------|
| Slow-release granules | Coated granules release over time | 3-6 months | Mixed into soil at planting |
| Liquid concentrate | Dilute and apply with watering | Immediate, repeat weekly | Regular feeding during growth |
| Organic granules | Broken down by soil biology | 2-4 weeks | Top-dressing, gentle feeding |
| Compost / worm castings | Slow nutrient release + biology | Ongoing | Mixed into soil or top-dressed |
| Foliar spray | Absorbed through leaves | Immediate | Quick micronutrient fix |

## NPK basics

| Nutrient | What it does | Deficiency signs |
|----------|-------------|-----------------|
| N (nitrogen) | Leaf and stem growth | Pale/yellow lower leaves, slow growth |
| P (phosphorus) | Root development, flowering, fruiting | Purple-tinged leaves, poor flowering |
| K (potassium) | Overall health, disease resistance, fruit quality | Brown leaf edges, weak stems |

### NPK ratios by stage

| Growth stage | N:P:K ratio | Example |
|-------------|-------------|---------|
| Seedling | Minimal or none | No feeding for first 2-3 weeks |
| Vegetative growth | High N | 3-1-2 or balanced 1-1-1 |
| Flowering / fruiting | Lower N, higher P and K | 1-2-2 or 1-3-2 |
| Herbs (harvesting leaves) | Moderate balanced | 1-1-1, avoid over-feeding |

## Feeding schedule by crop stage

### Seedlings (0-4 weeks after germination)
- Weeks 0-2: no feeding (seed reserves + seed-starting mix is enough)
- Weeks 3-4: start with 1/4 strength liquid fertilizer once a week

### Young plants (after pricking out, 4-8 weeks)
- 1/2 strength liquid fertilizer once a week
- Or slow-release granules mixed into new soil at pricking out

### Adult plants in containers
- **Option A:** Slow-release granules at planting → supplement with liquid every 2 weeks during fruiting
- **Option B:** Liquid fertilizer weekly at full strength
- Tomatoes and peppers are heavy feeders — increase to 2x/week during fruiting peak
- Herbs need less — every 2 weeks, half strength

### Late season
- Reduce feeding 2-3 weeks before expected end of harvest
- Stop feeding overwintering plants in early fall

## Container-specific considerations

| Issue | Why it matters | What to do |
|-------|---------------|------------|
| Nutrient leaching | Water drains through, taking nutrients | Feed more frequently but at lower concentration |
| Salt buildup | Fertilizer salts accumulate in soil | Flush with clean water monthly (water until drainage runs clear) |
| Limited root zone | Roots can't forage beyond container | Consistent, regular feeding is more critical than in ground |
| pH drift | Repeated fertilizing can shift pH | Monitor if plants show deficiency despite feeding |

## Organic vs mineral

Both work in containers. Key differences:

| Factor | Organic | Mineral |
|--------|---------|---------|
| Speed | Slower release, gentler | Immediate availability |
| Overfeeding risk | Lower | Higher — easy to burn roots |
| Soil biology | Supports beneficial microbes | Neutral or mildly suppressive |
| Cost | Often higher | Often lower |
| Smell | Some products have odor (fish, manure) | Usually odorless |

For containers, a combination often works best: slow-release organic base + liquid mineral supplement during heavy fruiting.
