# Common Gardening Mistakes

## Seedling care

### Misting instead of watering
- **Problem:** Misting wets the surface but doesn't reach roots. Seedlings stall — cotyledons only, no true leaves, thin stems.
- **Fix:** Water into the soil with a syringe, squeeze bottle, or small watering can. Soil should be moist through the full depth of the container. Let the top dry slightly between waterings.
- **Signs:** Stunted growth for weeks, no true leaves despite adequate light and temperature.

### Keeping germination temperature after sprouting
- **Problem:** Seeds need 25-30°C to germinate, but seedlings need 18-24°C (varies by crop). Leaving seedlings at germination temperature causes rapid, weak stem growth.
- **Fix:** Move to cooler location as soon as sprouts appear.
- **Reference temperatures (seedlings):**
  - Tomatoes: 20-22°C day / 16-18°C night
  - Peppers/chili: 22-24°C day / 18-20°C night
  - Basil: 20-22°C day / 18°C+ night

### Loosening soil around seedlings
- **Problem:** Seedling roots are in the top 1-2 cm. Loosening soil tears them, stunting or killing the plant.
- **Fix:** Don't touch the soil around seedlings. If surface crusts — water gently to soften, or use a lighter soil mix next time.

### Leaving sprouts in darkness
- **Problem:** Seeds germinate in dark/warm place, but sprouts need light immediately. Even 1-2 days in darkness after sprouting causes severe leggy growth.
- **Fix:** Check daily. Move to light the same day sprouts appear. Grow lights should run 14-16 hours/day.

### Sowing too early
- **Problem:** Sowing basil or tomatoes 12-16 weeks before last frost (instead of 6-8) means seedlings outgrow their space, become root-bound, and weaken before transplant.
- **Fix:** Follow the seasonal formulas. Earlier is not always better — the right timing produces healthier plants.

### Grow light too far from plants
- **Problem:** LED at 50+ cm causes stretching even with 16 hours. Seedlings lean and develop thin, weak stems.
- **Fix:** Keep LED grow lights at 25-40 cm from plant tops. Adjust as plants grow. If stems stretch — lower the lamp.

## Outdoor garden

### Pruning stone fruit in spring
- **Problem:** Cherry and plum pruned during sap flow are highly susceptible to silver leaf disease (Chondrostereum purpureum) and bacterial canker.
- **Fix:** Prune stone fruit in July-August only. Remove dead/broken branches any time. See `references/pruning.md`.

### Planting in raw clay without amendment
- **Problem:** Clay soil compacts, drains poorly, and forms surface crust. Roots can't penetrate, plants drown or starve.
- **Fix:** Add compost to planting holes and beds. For long-term improvement: lime (calcium) in fall, cover crops, organic mulch.

### Not mulching after transplant
- **Problem:** Bare soil (especially clay) dries, cracks, and overheats in sun. Roots exposed, water evaporates quickly.
- **Fix:** Mulch with straw, grass clippings, or wood chips 5-10 cm thick after transplanting. Keep mulch 3-5 cm away from stems.

### Harvesting young asparagus
- **Problem:** Asparagus needs 2-3 years to establish root system. Harvesting in year 1-2 weakens the plant permanently.
- **Fix:** Let all spears grow to fern stage for the first 2-3 years. Harvest lightly in year 3, fully from year 4.

## Container growing

### No drainage holes
- **Problem:** Water pools at the bottom, roots rot, plant dies. This is the #1 container kill.
- **Fix:** Every container must have drainage holes. Drill them if needed. At least 3-5 holes, 8-12 mm each.

### Underestimating container size
- **Problem:** Large plants (indeterminate tomatoes, cucumbers) in small pots become root-bound, need constant watering, and underperform.
- **Fix:** See `references/containers.md` for recommended volumes. Bigger is almost always better for fruiting crops.

### Forgetting containers freeze through
- **Problem:** Perennials in containers (boxwood, strawberry) can die in winter because containers freeze solid — unlike ground soil which insulates below frost line.
- **Fix:** Insulate containers (bubble wrap, styrofoam), shelter from wind, or bring to unheated garage/shed (+2-8°C).
