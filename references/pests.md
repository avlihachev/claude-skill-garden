# Pests and Diseases Reference

## Common container pests

### Aphids
- **Symptoms:** Clusters of small soft-bodied insects on new growth, sticky residue (honeydew), curled leaves
- **Crops affected:** Nearly all, especially peppers, basil, tomatoes
- **Prevention:** Inspect regularly, avoid over-fertilizing with nitrogen, encourage airflow
- **Treatment:**
  - Mild: spray off with strong water jet
  - Moderate: insecticidal soap spray (repeat every 3-5 days)
  - Severe: neem oil solution

### Whitefly
- **Symptoms:** Tiny white flying insects under leaves, sticky honeydew, sooty mold
- **Crops affected:** Tomatoes, peppers, basil
- **Prevention:** Yellow sticky traps near plants, inspect undersides of leaves
- **Treatment:**
  - Yellow sticky traps to reduce population
  - Insecticidal soap on undersides of leaves
  - Neem oil as systemic treatment

### Spider mites
- **Symptoms:** Fine webbing on leaf undersides, stippled/bronzed leaves, dry conditions favor them
- **Crops affected:** Tomatoes, peppers, strawberries, herbs
- **Prevention:** Maintain humidity, mist plants regularly, avoid dry hot conditions
- **Treatment:**
  - Increase humidity around plants
  - Spray undersides of leaves with water
  - Insecticidal soap or neem oil
  - In severe cases: miticide

### Fungus gnats
- **Symptoms:** Small dark flies around soil surface, larvae in top soil layer, seedlings may wilt
- **Crops affected:** All container plants, especially seedlings
- **Prevention:** Let top layer of soil dry between waterings, use sand or grit mulch on surface
- **Treatment:**
  - Yellow sticky traps for adults
  - Let soil dry out more between waterings
  - Hydrogen peroxide drench (1 part 3% H2O2 to 4 parts water)
  - Bacillus thuringiensis israelensis (BTI) granules in water

### Slugs and snails (outdoor containers)
- **Symptoms:** Irregular holes in leaves, slime trails, nighttime damage
- **Crops affected:** Lettuce, basil, strawberries, seedlings
- **Prevention:** Copper tape around containers, raise containers off ground, water in morning not evening
- **Treatment:**
  - Hand-pick at dusk
  - Beer traps
  - Iron phosphate pellets (safe for organic growing)

## Common diseases

### Damping off (seedling disease)
- **Symptoms:** Seedlings collapse at soil line, stem thin and brown at base, affects young seedlings only
- **Cause:** Fungal pathogens in overly wet, poorly ventilated conditions
- **Prevention:**
  - Use sterile seed-starting mix
  - Don't overwater — mist, don't drench
  - Ensure air circulation (remove covers after germination)
  - Avoid overcrowding seedlings
  - Keep temperature in optimal range (not too cold)
- **Treatment:** No cure for affected seedlings. Remove dead plants, improve conditions for survivors, resow if needed.

### Powdery mildew
- **Symptoms:** White powdery coating on leaves, starts as spots, spreads
- **Crops affected:** Cucumbers, squash, herbs, strawberries
- **Prevention:** Good air circulation, avoid wetting leaves, don't overcrowd
- **Treatment:**
  - Remove affected leaves
  - Baking soda spray (1 tbsp per liter water + drop of dish soap)
  - Neem oil preventive spray
  - Improve airflow

### Blossom end rot
- **Symptoms:** Dark, sunken, leathery patch on bottom of fruit (tomatoes, peppers)
- **Cause:** Calcium deficiency caused by inconsistent watering (not lack of calcium in soil)
- **Prevention:**
  - Consistent, even watering — critical in containers
  - Mulch soil surface to retain moisture
  - Don't let containers dry out completely between waterings
- **Treatment:** Remove affected fruits. Fix watering schedule. Usually resolves on its own with consistent moisture.

### Late blight
- **Symptoms:** Dark water-soaked spots on leaves, white mold underneath, brown spots on fruit, rapid plant death
- **Crops affected:** Tomatoes, potatoes
- **Prevention:** Good airflow, avoid overhead watering, remove lower leaves touching soil
- **Treatment:**
  - Remove affected leaves immediately
  - Copper-based fungicide as preventive
  - In severe cases: remove entire plant to prevent spread

## Container-specific disease considerations

- **Overwatering** is the #1 cause of disease in containers — more than pests
- **Good drainage** prevents most root rot and fungal issues
- **Airflow** between containers prevents mildew and mold
- **Clean containers** — sanitize reused pots with dilute bleach before new season
- **Don't reuse soil** from diseased plants
