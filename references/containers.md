# Containers Reference

## Recommended volumes by crop

| Crop type | Minimum | Optimum | Notes |
|-----------|---------|---------|-------|
| Dwarf/bush tomatoes | 5 L | 7-8 L | Balcony varieties |
| Determinate tomatoes | 8 L | 10-12 L | Standard compact varieties |
| Indeterminate tomatoes | 12 L | 15-20 L | Need support (stake/cage) |
| Peppers (compact) | 5 L | 7-8 L | Jalapeño-type |
| Peppers (large bush) | 7 L | 10 L | Habanero, bell peppers |
| Basil (single plant) | 1 L | 2-3 L | Can share a container |
| Basil (bush) | 3 L | 5 L | |
| Herbs (single) | 1-2 L | 3 L | Dill, parsley, cilantro |
| Lettuce (single head) | 2 L | 3-5 L | |
| Cucumbers | 10 L | 15-20 L | Need support |
| Strawberries | 3 L | 5 L per plant | Can use long planters |
| Boxwood / ornamental shrubs | 10 L | 15-20 L | Depends on plant size |

## Container materials

| Material | Pros | Cons | Best for |
|----------|------|------|----------|
| Plastic pots | Lightweight, cheap, retains moisture | Can overheat in sun, less aesthetic | Most crops |
| Plastic buckets | Large volume, cheap, available | Need drainage holes drilled | Tomatoes, cucumbers |
| Grow bags | Good drainage, air pruning of roots | Dry out faster, tip over | Tomatoes, peppers |
| Fabric bags | Excellent drainage, air pruning | Dry out very fast | Warm climates |
| Soil bags (direct planting) | Great insulation, cheap, large volume | Not reusable, looks informal | Tomatoes, squash |
| Recycled bottles | Free, good for seedlings | Small volume only | Seedling stage |
| Egg cartons | Free, biodegradable | Very small, dry fast | Seed starting only |
| Terracotta / ceramic | Breathable, aesthetic, stable | Heavy, expensive, fragile, dries fast | Herbs, ornamentals |

## Drainage

- **Every container must have drainage holes** — no exceptions
- Hole size: 8-12 mm, at least 3-5 holes per container
- Optional: 2-3 cm layer of expanded clay (LECA) at the bottom
- Saucers: useful indoors, but empty standing water after 30 min

## Temperature management

| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Root overheating (dark containers in sun) | Wrap with light fabric, use light-colored containers, double-pot |
| Cold nights (spring/fall) | Wrap with fleece/agrotextile, move indoors, group containers together |
| Frozen substrate (overwintering) | Insulate with bubble wrap or styrofoam, shelter from wind |

## Overwintering containers (perennials, boxwood)

- Location: cool (+2-8°C), ventilated, frost-protected (unheated garage, shed, covered balcony)
- NOT in warm rooms — disrupts dormancy
- Water sparingly: every 3-4 weeks, check substrate moisture
- Watch for physiological drought in late winter: sun warms foliage but roots are in frozen substrate → water when substrate begins to thaw
- Spring acclimation: gradual exposure to outdoor conditions over 2-3 weeks to prevent sun scorch
